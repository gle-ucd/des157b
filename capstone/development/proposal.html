Capstone Proposal: Altering Perspectives on Climate Change

Background

There are many things in the world to be concerned about, from the little things to those that are too incomprehensible to address. Political polarization, world hunger, wars, climate change, etc., are constant worries for most people. It is easy to see how pessimism and stress can fester and take hold under such circumstances. While some issues can't be addressed directly, or at all, that doesn't mean there isn't hope. This project doesn't aim to fix these major problems, but rather, it is meant to alter the perspectives people have on them. Specifically, it is meant to address one of the major perspectives people hold in society.

Climate anxiety is a prominent mental model for many people. It was evident from the brief research conducted for a previous assignment, as well as firsthand discussions with those around me, that there is a sense of hopelessness when it comes to climate change. Many people have a deeply pessimistic view of the future overall. This future always, in some shape or form, consists of a world where climate change has devastated the human population and the environment. To most people, the effects of climate change are inevitable; nothing can be done to alter this reality.

Goal

This project aims to alter the hopeless perspective many people have about the future of climate change. It seeks to alleviate the burden this mental model places on the audience, turning pessimism into optimism about the future. Consequently, this optimism can help foster climate change activism and action.

Audience

The goal of the project can only be achieved if it reaches a wide range of people, from young to old. The general public is the overall audience. Those with climate anxiety, climate activists, and those who have given up on combating climate change are the project's main audience. For climate activists, the hope is to motivate this subset of the audience to continue their efforts. As for the rest, the hope is to inspire them to take action. Overall, the focus of this project is alleviating the mental fatigue surrounding climate change.

Methods & Features

In a general sense, this project is similar to a data visualization project. Key efforts taken to combat climate change will be the primary focus displayed. The intention is not to overload the audience with too much information, but rather to showcase only the necessary information required to alter this mental model. There will likely not be graphs in this project, but showing a substantial number of projects and efforts undertaken is important.

Compiling these efforts into a visually appealing and engaging design is imperative to the success of the project. The Granim.js and anime.js libraries will be used to make the data points feel more dynamic. Other libraries that can help improve the user experience and interaction, such as jQuery (drag and drop) and Animate On Scroll, will also be considered.
